Without shadows and/or ambient occlusion (i.e., using a OpenGL-like local shading model), the FIU dataset looks like this (click on image to see larger version):

center: FIU with ray traced shadows
Simply adding shadows already greatly enhances the user's perception of depth (e.g., the relative positions of stream lines and base geometry, the actual shape of the geometry, etc).

right: FIU with OSPRay's Ambient Occlusion Renderer
Ambient Occlusion further increases perception of depth. In OSPRay, enabling (true, ray-cast) Ambient Occlusion is as simple as selecting the "ao" renderer.
